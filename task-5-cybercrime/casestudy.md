# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study

## Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

---

As a Student Digital Ambassador at VIT Bhopal University, one
of the most important things I want my batchmates to be aware
of is UPI fraud. It is one of the fastest growing cybercrimes 
in India and it is targeting college students more than ever. 
Here is what you need to know.

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI fraud is a type of online financial crime where scammers 
trick people into sending money or revealing their UPI PIN 
through fake calls, messages or payment requests. Unlike 
traditional bank fraud, UPI fraud happens within seconds 
and is nearly impossible to reverse once the money is gone.

## How it Happens – Step by Step

1. You receive a call from someone claiming to be a bank 
   representative, customer care agent or a buyer for 
   something you posted online for sale

2. They sound convincing — they may already know your name, 
   bank name or other basic details

3. They tell you to scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN 
   to "receive" a payment or "verify" your account

4. The moment you enter your PIN or approve the request, 
   money is instantly debited from your account

5. The scammer disconnects immediately and the money is 
   moved across multiple accounts within minutes

## Who is Being Targeted?

As a Digital Ambassador I want to be honest — it is people 
like us. First year students buying and selling on OLX or 
Instagram, people who just got their first UPI account and 
are not fully familiar with how it works, and anyone who 
is in a hurry or under pressure during the call.

## Real Life Scenario

A first year student posted his old laptop for sale on OLX. 
A buyer contacted him and said he would send payment via UPI. 
He sent a QR code saying "scan this to receive money." The 
student scanned it, entered his UPI PIN and instead of 
receiving money Rs. 15,000 was instantly debited from his 
account. By the time he realised what happened the scammer 
had already switched off his phone.

This is not a rare case. This happens every day across 
college campuses in India.

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss with very little chance of recovery
- Victims feel embarrassed and often do not report it
- Mental stress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Repeated targeting once scammers know you are vulnerable

## What You Should Do

If this happens to you or someone you know — report it 
immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal at 
**www.cybercrime.gov.in** or **call helpline 1930**. Do not wait, 
do not feel embarrassed. The faster you report it the 
better the chances of recovery.
